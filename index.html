Essay

And suddenly I was standing on the podium in front of thousands of people inside the United Nations building. I looked around because something felt different. Then I realized why. It was the first time I had ever received a standing ovation. Thousands of people were cheering for me. After years of hard work, discipline, and self control, every sacrifice felt worth it. While many people my age spent their free time playing games, I often chose to study longer, train harder, and keep improving. Those choices helped me earn second place at the United Nations Games Chess Tournament. Through chess I learned that the hardest opponent was never the person sitting across from me. It was myself. Soon after that, I won first place at an IBJJF Brazilian Jiu Jitsu Open. Then I woke up. I was not waking up from a dream. I was waking up from one stage of my life. My years in the United States had come to an end. Leaving the country that helped me grow both academically and personally was one of the hardest things I have ever done. It was there that I learned how to challenge myself, stay focused, and never give up. Returning to Kazakhstan was difficult, but it also gave me time to think about everything I had achieved and where I wanted to continue my journey. Studying at PS IS 78Q and later at Manhattan Village Academy helped me grow both mentally and academically. At Manhattan Village Academy, I was recognized as one of the top 20 freshman students and received the Principal's Award for Academic Excellence for maintaining an average above 95 percent. I also earned a Certificate of Academic Excellence in Spanish. Outside the classroom, I competed in national and international chess and Brazilian Jiu Jitsu tournaments, earning first place at the New York Spring International Open and second place at the United Nations Games Chess Tournament. I also played varsity soccer and completed the Artificial Intelligence program at the City College of New York STEM Institute, where I received the Best Project Award and the Leadership Award. These experiences taught me to stay calm under pressure, work with others, and keep improving. They also helped me discover my interest in computer science, economics, and applied mathematics. I chose CIS because its internationally recognized Canadian curriculum emphasizes critical thinking, collaboration, and academic excellence. After studying in the United States, I value an environment that encourages curiosity, independence, and personal growth, and I believe CIS offers that opportunity while allowing me to stay close to my family. I hope to contribute to the school through my strong work ethic, leadership, and willingness to learn. I know I will not succeed every time, but I will never give up. That is the mindset I will bring to the CIS community.
